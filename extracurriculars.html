Research


As of January 2022, I have had the honor of being an undergraduate research assistant for Elizabeth Ankrah at Dr. Gillian Hayes's Social and Technological Action Research Group (STAR) Lab.


Research Area

The focus of our research is on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Health Informatics, Health Equity, and Community Health. As part of our ongoing work with childhood cancer survivors, we have been co-designing socio-technical interventions that support their lifestyle and long-term chronic illness management.


My Collaboration

  • Facilitate 2-hour-long co-design sessions where participants talk about their experiences, what resources they want, and devise technological solutions that address their unmet needs.
  • Take detailed notes about what occurs during meetings with cancer survivor participants, which includes dialogue, questions asked, and new information.
  • Meet weekly with 2 Ph.D. researchers to recap information learned from previous meetings with participants and plan future ones.

CMD-IT/ACM Richard Tapia Conference 2022


What I did


I'm honored to have been selected as a scholar for the national CMD-IT/ACM Richard Tapia Conference 2022! Three days of conference activities took place in Washington DC from September 13-16. During my time there, I had the opportunity to participate in the ACM Student Research Competition, which offers undergraduate and graduate students an opportunity to present their original research to a panel of judges and attendees at well-known ACM conferences and co-sponsored events.

I am blessed to have placed second in the undergraduate category. I would not have been able to accomplish this without the constant support of my mentors: Elizabeth Ankrah, Arpita Bhattacharya, Emani Dotch, Gillian Hayes, and the members of STAR Lab.
